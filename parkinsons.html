― DESIGN FOR PARKINSONS


Overview

This is a team project for the UCSD Design Competition hosted by UCSD Electrical and Computer Engineering Department and sponsored by IBM. The goal is to improve the quality of life for parkinsons's Disease patient and caregiver.

Position

UX Researcher, UX/UI Designer (April → Ongoing)

Skills

UX Research, Data Collection, UI Design, Interview Techniques

Tools

Figma

User Research

Research Methodology

To better understand parkinsons’s disease, we attended 3 info session to understand Parkinsons’s disease. To understand the parkinsons’s patients and caregivers needs, we conducted interview 10 patients and caregivers.

Later, I had the opportunity to visit a patient house and have a deeper conversation and understanding of the patient and caregiver.




Core Problem

In the problem finding stage, we found three major problems, patients forget to eat pills on time, patient without monitoring are at higher risk of accidents and visuospatial cognition degeneration. After talking to more patients and caregiver, we decided to focus on the problem that patients forget to eat pills on time because this is a problem that they face on a daily basis.

Problem that they did not eat pill on time could be caused by

  1. Patients genuinely forget
  2. Patients thought that they had eaten the pill earlier
  3. Setting up time interval for different day is troublesome


Challenge

How to improve the patients experience in remembering to take pills?



Redesign

Constantly ideating, iterating and validating

For the solution, we brainstormed our ideas and sketched as many prototypes we could come up with. Since we all had different ideas, each of us drew the prototype we had in mind, and then combined our idea into the best version. The sketches helped us come to an agreement on the redesign.

Final Solution

A synced system using several devices to remind patient and caregiver of prescription time, with an app, a pill box, and a bracelet.
